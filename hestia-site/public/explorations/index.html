Site 01 — “Thursday, 6pm” — design variations

Same site. Same words.
Three different skins.

The live site is settled — the door‑slide reveal (“It’s 6pm.” / “You’re home.” / “Nothing is on fire.”) into “Hestia already knew what you’d need,” word for word. These three pages keep that exact copy and that exact pinned‑scroll mechanic, and only change the visual language — typography, palette, panel shape, imagery treatment — so you can compare how the same promise reads in different clothes. Baseline for comparison: /thursday (or /) in the live app.

A / 03

Fraunces (black) + IBM Plex Mono + Source Serif 4

Heavier type · rubber‑stamp motif · inkier red

A — Stamped Ledger

Fraunces goes to black weight and drops the italic. A circular “Verified” stamp lands on the reveal, dashed perforation lines mark the doors’ inner edges, and the accent shifts to an inkier, more saturated red. Reads like a verified record more than a warm invitation.

Rounded bento panels · floating gutter · sage accent

B — Soft Modern

The doors become two fully‑rounded floating panels with a visible gap, the kitchen photo sits inside a soft bento frame instead of going edge‑to‑edge, and the accent moves from clay to a muted sage. Onest + Albert Sans reads calmer — closer to a considerate home‑services product than a magazine spread.

Archivo Black + Archivo

Near‑black · all‑caps poster type · grayscale photography

C — High-Contrast Poster

The palette drops to near‑black with one desaturated ochre rule, headlines go to Archivo Black in all caps at poster scale, and the kitchen photography is pushed toward grayscale. Hard edges, no rounded corners. Reads more “a service that means business” than “warm welcome.”
