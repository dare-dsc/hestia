## Session Overview

**Topic:** On-demand domestic services platform (chefs + housekeepers) - exploring current belief systems, assumptions, and mental models around domestic help and gig economy home services

**Goals:**
- Uncover existing beliefs about domestic help and home services
- Identify assumptions we're challenging or reinforcing with this platform
- Surface mental barriers or opportunities in the market
- Understand current paradigms around gig economy domestic work

### Session Setup

**The Core Concept:**
An on-demand platform connecting people who need immediate domestic help (cooking, cleaning, household tasks) with professional chefs and housekeepers who can work flexibly - similar to how Uber connects riders with drivers. The platform enables hourly wages and flexible scheduling without requiring full-time employment commitments.

**The Pain Point:**
That overwhelming moment when you're exhausted from work/school, the laundry has piled up, dishes are dirty, kids' clothes need attention - and you have a busy schedule. You wish you could just call someone for help without having to employ them full-time.

**The Opportunity:**
Just as Uber provides transportation services without requiring car ownership, this platform would provide domestic services without requiring employment contracts - making professional help accessible, flexible, and immediate.

### Market Context Focus: Kenya

**Research Questions:**
- What do people in Kenya currently believe about domestic help?
- How do they choose housekeepers/chefs today?
- What are the current systems and processes in place?
- What mental models drive current hiring practices?
- What trust mechanisms exist?
- What are the existing payment and employment structures?

## Technique Selection

**Approach:** Progressive Technique Flow
**Journey Design:** Systematic development from expansive exploration to actionable insights

**Progressive Techniques:**

- **Phase 1 - Expansive Exploration:** What If Scenarios — challenge every assumption about domestic help in Kenya, generate maximum creative breadth (30-40 raw ideas)
- **Phase 2 - Pattern Recognition:** Assumption Reversal — extract and flip the core beliefs/assumptions uncovered in Phase 1 to reveal Hestia's disruptive opportunities
- **Phase 3 - Idea Development:** Role Playing — develop the strongest opportunities through multiple stakeholder lenses (professional, housekeeper, agency, first-time user)
- **Phase 4 - Action Planning:** SCAMPER Method — systematically refine top concepts through 7 innovation lenses into actionable platform directions

**Journey Rationale:** The session goals are explicitly about beliefs, assumptions, and mental models — this progression is designed to surface them first (Phase 1), invert them into opportunities (Phase 2), ground them in human reality (Phase 3), and crystallize them into product innovation (Phase 4).
