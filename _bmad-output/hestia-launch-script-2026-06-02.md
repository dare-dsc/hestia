**Created:** 2026-06-04

## Story Information

**Story Type:** Conversion Ad — Pain Recognition + Reveal + Qualified Invitation

**Framework Used:** Recognition → Real Cost → Reveal → Invitation
*(Borrowed Urban Company's reveal structure; adapted to Hestia's emotional USP instead of a speed USP)*

**Optimization target:** First paying customer — a qualified reply from someone ready to act *this week*, not just pain agreement

**Purpose:** Make the right person feel so seen they reply *and* signal they are ready to pay. Filter out the curious. Find the ready.

**Target Audience:** Busy Nairobi apartment dwellers — young professionals, bachelors, couples, single parents — carrying domestic overwhelm and currently patching it with incomplete solutions (bureau, mama fua, takeaway, nothing).

---

## Story Structure

### Opening Hook

*"You come home. The sink is full. The clothes are on the chair. And you just sit down — because where do you even start?"*

The hook opens mid-scene, no introduction, no company name. The customer is already in the story before they realize it.

### Core Narrative

The story moves in one direction: from the pain the customer carries alone → to the moment they realize they don't have to.

### Key Story Beats

1. **The wrong question** — reframe: the problem is not "I need a cleaner", it is "my house is another job waiting"
2. **The real cost** — the Saturday cycle: the weight, not the mess
3. **Why existing solutions fail** — bureau, referral, mama fua, takeaway — all solve one piece, none take the weight off
4. **The reveal** — Hestia is not a bureau or listing; it is a home relief service; the bundle; *"the house feels like yours again"*
5. **The qualified invitation** — five homes, this week, one clear price, no commitment — *"not Saturday, this week"*

### Emotional Arc

- **Start:** Recognition — *"the problem is not what I thought it was — it is the weight"*
- **Middle:** Frustration validated — *"all my current solutions only solve one piece"*
- **Reveal:** Relief — *"someone built the thing that takes the whole weight off"*
- **End:** Urgency — *"not someday — this week"*

### Resolution / Call to Action

A specific, time-bound, investment-signalling invitation:

> *"One visit. One clear price. No commitment after. If you want your home back — not Saturday, this week — reply to this message."*

**Why "one clear price" and not a specific number:**
Naming the price before value is felt triggers calculation before desire. "One clear price" signals paid without anchoring. The person who wants to know the number will ask — that question is the conversation that becomes a booking.

## Story Elements Analysis

### Character / Voice

**Dare** — not a salesperson, not a company spokesperson. A person who saw a problem and chose to fix it. The voice is calm, direct, warm. No hustle energy. No desperation. The energy is: *I know what I am building and I am speaking to you specifically because I believe you need it.*

Do not perform this. Say it like you are talking to one tired person you already know.

### Conflict / Tension

The core conflict is internal, not external. It is not *"the bureaus are bad."* It is the customer's own private war with their home — the guilt, the procrastination, the slow accumulation of things they keep deferring.

That internal tension is more powerful than any external villain. The customer already feels it. Your job is just to name it out loud.

### Transformation / Change

The transformation the video promises is not a clean house. It is:

> *"You come home and the house feels like yours again."*

That line carries the full weight of the Hestia promise — dignity, relief, continuity. Not just a mopped floor.

### Emotional Touchpoints

| Moment in script | Emotion triggered |
|---|---|
| "Your house is another job waiting for you" | Reframe — *that is exactly it* |
| "Dishes. Laundry. The floor. No food." | Specificity — *they know my home* |
| "That cycle — that is what costs you. Not the mess. The weight." | Deep recognition — the invisible burden named |
| "Saturday comes. You're tired. You spend your rest day..." | The painful truth they have normalized |
| "All of them solve one piece. None take the weight off." | Frustration validated — current solutions are incomplete |
| "Not a bureau. Not another listing." | Differentiation lands before the pitch |
| "The house feels like yours again." | Longing — they feel what has been missing |
| "Not Saturday. This week." | Urgency — the cycle stops now, not later |

### Key Messages

1. Hestia is not a bureau — it is a home relief service
2. The product is the feeling, not the worker
3. The founder is personally involved — real accountability
4. No commitment required to try
5. You do not have to carry this alone

>

>
> *If that's you, reply to this message. Even just a yes. We'll reach out directly — no pressure, just a conversation."*

### Extended Version — Door conversation / caretaker introduction context

## Usage Guidelines

### Best Channels

| Channel | How to use it |
|---|---|
| **Building WhatsApp group** | Primary. Caretaker sends with personal vouching message (see below). |
| **WhatsApp Status** | Dare posts as a status — residents who have Dare's number will see it |
| **Personal DM** | Caretaker forwards directly to 2–3 residents they know personally have this problem |
| **Face to face** | If a resident stops to talk, use Beat 3–5 verbally. No need for the full script. |

### Audience Considerations

- **Bachelors and single professionals** — the mess scene will hit hard. The Saturday normalization is their life.
- **Young couples both working** — the tension of who should handle the home adds another layer. Hestia removes the argument.
- **New parents** — the overwhelm is doubled. The "home feels like yours again" line lands differently here — deeper relief.
- **Diaspora returnees** — they have experienced structured home services abroad. They will respond faster and ask about pricing immediately. Have the Home Reset Trial ready for them.

### Tone and Voice Notes

- **Calm, not urgent.** Never sound like you need them. You are offering relief, not chasing revenue.
- **Specific, not general.** "The sink. The clothes on the chair." Not "your home is messy." Specificity creates recognition.
- **Personal, not corporate.** Say "I" not "we" in the first three beats. "We" enters when you introduce Hestia. This keeps the trust anchored in a person before it transfers to a brand.
- **Stop when the script ends.** No "thank you for watching." No wave. The restraint signals confidence.

### Adaptation Suggestions

- If filming feels uncomfortable, record the voice note version first — hear yourself say the words out loud before doing it on camera. The camera version will be more natural after.
- If the building WhatsApp group does not exist yet, ask the caretaker if there is another channel residents use (estate notice board, Facebook group, direct WhatsApp broadcast list).
- Once you have 2–3 replies, film a second version that says *"a few people have already reached out — if you've been thinking about it, now is a good time."* Social proof changes the dynamic completely.

## The Caretaker Introduction Message

**Send this to the caretaker first. Ask them to send it in the group before or alongside the video.**

> *"Habari residents. I want to share something from someone I have spoken to personally — a person called Dare who is building a home service for people in buildings like ours. It is called Hestia. They are not a bureau. They are starting small and working directly with a few homes here first. I think it is worth watching. — [Caretaker name]"*

The caretaker's name at the end is the trust transfer. Do not skip it.

---

## The Follow-Up Message (When Someone Replies)

When a resident replies "yes" or asks a question — do not send a brochure. Do not send a price list. Send this:

> *"Thank you for reaching out. I'm Dare — I made that video. Can I ask you one question: when life gets busy, what's the first thing that starts piling up at home for you? I want to understand what would actually make a difference before we talk about anything else."*

That question does three things:
1. It proves you are a real person, not a bot
2. It starts the pain investigation conversation
3. It positions Hestia as a listener before a seller

Everything after that — the package, the price, the trial offer — follows from what they say.

## Next Steps

### Refinement Opportunities

- Once you have filmed the video, watch it once with the sound off. Does your face and body say *calm and confident* or *nervous and selling*? Reshoot if needed — it takes five minutes.
- Test the medium version text message with one person you trust before sending to the group. Does it read like you or like a company? Adjust accordingly.

### Additional Versions Needed (After First Replies)

1. **The social proof version** — *"Three people in this building have already booked a trial. Here is what one of them said after."* Film after first completed trial.
2. **The worker story version** — *"This is [Partner name]. She is the person who will come to your home."* Humanizes the service, answers the trust question before it is asked.
3. **The founder follow-up** — a short voice note to anyone who replied but has not yet committed. More personal than a text.

### Testing / Feedback Plan

After the video goes out, measure these:

| Signal | What it means |
|---|---|
| Replies in first 24 hours | Message is landing. The pain resonated. |
| Views but no replies | People watched but did not act. The ask may be too high or the pain not sharp enough. Adjust the CTA. |
| Caretaker gets asked about it verbally | The video is circulating. Personal conversations are starting. |
| Someone asks about price immediately | High interest, low trust. Lead with the trial offer — no price until they feel the pain. |
| No views at all | The caretaker introduction did not land. Revisit the caretaker conversation before reposting. |

## Production Checklist — Before You Film

**Setup**
- [ ] iPhone in portrait (9:16), 4K if available
- [ ] Face a window — natural light hits your face from the front
- [ ] Background: real kitchen or living area, slightly lived-in — not a blank wall, not chaos
- [ ] Camera at eye level — prop on books or a cup if filming alone
- [ ] Lock exposure: press and hold on your face until AE/AF LOCK appears
- [ ] Wear something clean and simple — no logos, no distractions

**Delivery**
- [ ] Read the script out loud twice before filming — not to memorize, just to hear it
- [ ] Slow down at: *"That cycle — that is what costs you. Not the mess. The weight of it."* — pause after "weight"
- [ ] Slow down at: *"You come home, and the house feels like yours again."* — this is the reveal, let it land
- [ ] Speak to one person, not a crowd
- [ ] Stop when the last word ends — do not wave, do not say thank you

**CapCut Editing**
- [ ] Auto-captions on immediately — most viewers watch on mute
- [ ] Bold text overlay on: *"Not Saturday. This week."* — this is the filter line
- [ ] Warm colour grade: Film or Golden filter at 25–30% intensity
- [ ] Subtle background music: warm, understated, 15–20% volume — voice carries the video
- [ ] No logo intro, no outro animation — keep it human
- [ ] Total edited length: under 75 seconds
