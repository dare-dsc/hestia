Conversation Anchors

"I am not selling.
I am listening for pain.
My job is to help this person name
something they have already been feeling."

Open — Permission
"Hi, I'm Dare from Hestia. We're building a home-support service for busy people. I'm not here to sell anything today. Can I steal 10 minutes?"
If hesitant → "No pressure at all. Even a no gives me something useful."
Warm Up — 1 or 2 questions only
"How long have you been in this area?"  /  "You stay alone or with others?"
Then bridge → "Okay, let me ask you something real…"
Dig In — Go slow. Let them talk.
  • →
    "When you get home tired — what usually waits for you?" Don't suggest answers. Let them speak.
  • →
    "What piles up first when things get hectic?" If quiet, offer softly: "Dishes? Laundry? Cooking? Cleaning?"
  • →
    "How does it make you feel when the house is like that?" PAUSE. WAIT. Write their exact words — tired, ashamed, demotivated, "I just ignore it."
  • →
    "When it gets bad — what do you usually do?" Call someone? Order food? Do it yourself? Nothing?
  • →
    "What annoys you about that?"
The Reveal — Only after they have spoken

"This is exactly why I'm building Hestia. That backlog, that feeling — we want to remove it. Trusted help for cleaning, laundry, dishes, and simple home food. Not hotel food. Mother's touch. Chapati, rice stew, things that feel like home. The goal is simple: you come home and the house feels lighter."

— Pause. Watch their face. Their face is data. —
Close
  • →
    "Would that solve a real problem for you?"
  • →
    "What would you need to see before letting Hestia into your home?" Write every condition. Each one is a product feature.
  • →
    "We're doing a few first home trials — one reset, honest feedback. Would you try it?"
  • →
    "Do you know someone whose home is always overwhelmed? Friend, colleague, neighbour?" Ask this regardless of their answer. Always.
Reflect their words back before moving on — it makes them feel heard
