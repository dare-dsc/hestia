# Product Brief: Hestia

## Executive Summary

Every evening, millions of Kenyan households face the same invisible crisis: the dishes are unwashed, the children are home from school, the house hasn't been cleaned in a week, and there is no one to call. Full-time domestic help is out of reach. The bureaus are unreliable. The WhatsApp groups are a gamble. And asking a neighbour for a referral takes days you don't have.

Hestia is the on-demand domestic services platform that solves this — not with a listing, but with a promise. Like Uber, but for your home: open the app, book a verified domestic specialist, and someone arrives. Unlike every bureau and platform that came before it, Hestia earns on every service — not on every placement — which means Hestia's business model is built on trust that compounds, not transactions that disappear.

The domestic services market in Kenya is a high-volume, low-trust territory. Over 2 million Kenyans work as domestic workers. The digital trust layer is nascent. No operator has built training infrastructure, on-arrival verification, insurance, or recurring employer revenue at scale. The territory is open. Hestia is the Urban Company of East Africa.

---

## The Problem

Picture this: it's 6pm on a Thursday. You're stuck in traffic on Ngong Road. Your kids have been home for two hours. The dishes from last night are still in the sink. You haven't cleaned the flat since Sunday. Your live-in help left without notice last month. You've been cycling through bureau after bureau — paying KSh 2,000 each time for workers who don't show up, can't mop properly, or disappear after three weeks.

This is not an edge case. It is the daily reality of Kenya's growing middle class.

The dominant hiring channel — word-of-mouth referral — is trustworthy but does not scale. You can only hire from people your neighbour knows. Traditional walk-in bureaus charge upfront, conduct minimal vetting, and vanish after placement. Digital players like Msichana.co.ke have built a browse-and-choose profile layer, but earn KES 200 per placement and have no relationship with either party after day one.

The result: a market with structural distrust on both sides. Employers fear theft, inconsistency, and poor vetting. Workers fear exploitation, withheld wages, and unsafe working conditions. Everyone is losing.

**The cost of the status quo:**
- Employers spend an average of 4+ hours finding and vetting a new worker every time one leaves
- Worker turnover is extremely high — many employers cycle through 3–4 workers per year
- Domestic workers earn KES 6,000–8,000/month on average, with no portable reputation, no insurance, and no upward mobility
- No Kenyan operator currently offers insurance, guaranteed re-service, on-arrival verification, or subscription-based recurring service

---

## The Solution

Hestia is a domestic services brand — not a listing platform — that connects verified domestic specialists with households on a subscription basis.

**The core product (Phase 1): Hestia Clean**

A weekly cleaning subscription. The same verified domestic specialist arrives every week. If she cannot make it, a verified replacement is sent automatically. After every visit, the employer rates the service. The rating builds the worker's portable professional reputation over time.

**Booking experience:**
1. Employer books via WhatsApp or app — specifying date, time, and home size
2. Hestia matches a verified specialist from the local zone
3. Worker checks in on arrival via a verification selfie
4. Service is completed to Hestia's documented standard
5. Employer rates the session; worker's profile is updated

**The Hestia Trust Stack** — what every worker must pass before their first booking:
- DCI police clearance certificate
- Verified employment history (Hestia personally contacts every past employer)
- 3 verified references — digitized from Kenya's own village referral system
- Hestia practical skills assessment
- Insurance cover — automatic payout if anything goes wrong

**Pricing:** KES 1,500 per visit × 4 visits per month = KES 6,000/month subscription. Hestia takes a 20% commission per booking. Employers pay nothing upfront — no placement shock.

**Phase 2 product: Hestia Chef** — on-demand personal chefs for special occasions and recurring meal prep. Sourced from hotel and restaurant alumni. Launched once Hestia Clean has 200+ verified bookings.

## What Makes This Different

**1. We earn on every service, not every placement.**
Every Kenyan competitor — bureaus, digital platforms, agencies — earns once and disappears. Hestia earns every time the worker shows up. This recurring model funds a trust infrastructure no transactional player can afford to build.

**2. We compete against word-of-mouth, not against other bureaus.**
The real dominant channel in Kenya is not Msichana or BestCare — it is asking your neighbour. Hestia wins by being simultaneously more trustworthy and more convenient than a personal referral.

**3. Disintermediation is solved structurally, not contractually.**
Workers bypass platforms when the platform offers nothing they can't get directly. Hestia makes bypassing irrational: a worker's rating, insurance cover, premium client access, and portable reputation all live on the platform. Leaving means starting from zero.

**4. The brand is the trust layer.**
Employers do not book "a cleaner." They book Hestia. The brand absorbs the trust risk — the same model that made Molly Maid's franchises generate $855K/year in average unit revenue with a 90% repeat rate. Kenya has no domestic services brand. Hestia becomes it.

**5. We own the supply at source.**
The Western Kenya Certification Pipeline trains and certifies workers in Bungoma, Kakamega, and Vihiga before they arrive in Nairobi. Workers arrive pre-profiled, pre-vetted, with the Hestia badge. Hestia owns both ends of the market.

## Who We Don't Compete With

**Commercial cleaning companies (BestCare, Bidvest, Nationwide)**
These operators send teams of 2–3 people, charge KES 3,000–8,000 per session, and are designed for offices, hotels, and large commercial spaces. They solve for *clean*. They do not solve for *trust* — a different team shows up every time, you don't know who's coming, and the relationship ends when the session does. For heavy-duty deep cleans and commercial clients, they are the right product. For the weekly recurring household, they are too expensive and too impersonal to compete.

**Traditional bureaus and digital listing platforms (Msichana, walk-in bureaus)**
These earn on placement — KES 200 to KES 2,000 — and disappear after day one. They solve for *discovery*. They have no relationship with either party after the match is made, no accountability when things go wrong, and no recurring revenue to fund any trust infrastructure. Every bureau failure educates the market for Hestia.

**Hestia's true competitor is word-of-mouth referral** — which is free, personally trusted, and instantly available. Hestia wins not by being better than a bureau, but by being simultaneously more trustworthy and more convenient than asking your neighbour.

## Who This Serves

**Primary: The Nairobi Apartment Household**
Young dual-income professionals, 25–40, living in Kilimani, Lavington, and Westlands. Smaller apartments without servant quarters — dayworker preference. Highly frustrated with the bureau cycle. Digitally comfortable. Willing to pay a modest premium for reliability. Their core pain: *"I just want the same reliable person every week without the drama."*

**Secondary: The Diaspora Returner**
Kenyans returning from the UK, US, Canada, and Gulf states. Income KES 200K+/month. They have experienced professional domestic services abroad and are shocked by the informal baseline at home. High willingness to pay — 3–5x premium. The best early-adopter segment: already converted, just needs to be told Hestia exists.
Entry message: *"Your home deserves the same standard you had in London."*

**Supply side: The Experienced Domestic Worker**
Women, 22–45, with 2+ years of domestic work experience, currently underpaid and invisible to the formal market. Hestia offers: a full booking calendar, verified clients, guaranteed payment, a portable reputation, and insurance. The platform gets them more work at better rates. Bypassing Hestia destroys what they've built.

## Success Criteria

**90-day MVP targets:**
- 5 verified workers onboarded in Zone 1 (Kilimani or Lavington)
- 10 completed bookings with zero unresolved complaints
- 10 active monthly subscribers
- KES 12,000/month recurring revenue

**6-month targets:**
- 20 active subscribers in Zone 1
- Zone 1 self-sustaining (workers operate without founder supervision on every job)
- Worker satisfaction: 0 workers leaving the platform
- Employer NPS: 7+ out of 10

**12-month targets:**
- Zone 2 launched
- 50+ active subscribers
- Hestia Certification Programme launched
- Insurance layer live
- KES 60,000/month recurring revenue

## Scope

**In scope for v1 (Hestia Clean, Zone 1):**
- Domestic cleaning specialist booking — weekly subscription
- WhatsApp-first booking (no app required)
- 5-layer trust verification for all workers
- Post-visit rating system (WhatsApp-based)
- Guaranteed replacement if primary worker is unavailable
- Zone 1: Kilimani or Lavington, Nairobi

**Explicitly out of scope for v1:**
- Mobile app (WhatsApp is sufficient until 50+ subscribers)
- Hestia Chef product
- Zone 2 expansion
- Training programme (Phase 2)
- Insurance layer (Phase 2 — negotiate once recurring revenue exists)
- Childcare / elder care / gardening services

The worker who joins Hestia in Kakamega in 2026 has a professional career, a credit history built on her Hestia earnings, and NHIF contributions handled through the platform. The employer in Kilimani has not thought about finding a cleaner in three years.

Urban Company reached $137M in revenue in FY25 — its first profitable year — from the same founding insight applied to India. East Africa is at trust level 1–2 on a six-stage gradient. Every layer above it is unbuilt. Hestia builds it.
