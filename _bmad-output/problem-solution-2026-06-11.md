# Problem Solving Session: Formalizing Hestia's Legal Structure & Compliant HCP Onboarding

## 🎯 PROBLEM DEFINITION

### Initial Problem Statement

"We want to start hiring workers and asking for their credentials but we aren't sure under Kenyan law whether to register as a business or a company, and which is the best move at this stage. Are we allowed to take in personal details like birth certificates etc? We're about to onboard our first paid HCPs and aren't sure what we're allowed to take from workers under the law, or whether there's a policy/agreement they need to sign before being onboarded."

### Refined Problem Statement

Hestia is about to onboard its first **paid** Home Care Partners (HCPs), but currently operates without a registered legal entity and without a documented, lawful onboarding process.

Based on Hestia's own positioning — HCPs are supervised on first visits, trained, given a career ladder (Partner → Senior Partner → Supervisor → Trainer), and paid on a steady, reliable basis — the relationship is most accurately classified as **employment** under the Employment Act 2007's "substance over form" test, regardless of what label is used in a contract.

This classification means Hestia needs, before onboarding:

1. A **registered legal entity** capable of acting as a formal employer — i.e., able to obtain a KRA PIN and register for PAYE, NSSF, and SHIF — and ideally one that limits Duffy's personal liability.
2. A **lawful, consented onboarding process** for collecting and storing HCP personal/sensitive documents (national ID, birth certificate, Certificate of Good Conduct/DCI clearance) in line with the Data Protection Act 2019, embedded in a signed HCP onboarding agreement/policy.

The gap: neither the entity nor the onboarding agreement/data process exists yet, and onboarding of paid HCPs is imminent.

### Problem Context

- **Stage:** Founding-homes phase (per product brief), about to onboard the first *paid* HCPs — time-sensitive.
- **Working assumption (flag for advocate verification):** HCPs = employees in substance (control, supervision, training, career ladder, steady pay), not independent contractors/gig partners — see rationale above.
- **Two coupled decisions to resolve as one problem:**
  - **(A) Entity registration** — business name vs. limited company, sized for Hestia's current stage but capable of formal employer registration (KRA PIN, NSSF, SHIF) and ideally shielding Duffy from personal liability.
  - **(B) Onboarding compliance** — what personal documents can lawfully be collected from HCPs, what consent/safeguards apply under the Data Protection Act 2019, and what the signed onboarding agreement/policy must cover.
- **Stakeholders:** Duffy (founder, decision-maker, potential personal liability bearer), first cohort of paid HCPs (employees + data subjects), founding-home clients (expect verified, trustworthy Partners per the Trust Architecture).
- **Jurisdiction/legal framework:** Companies Act 2015 & Business Registration Service (entity choice), Employment Act 2007 (employment contracts, statutory deductions), Data Protection Act 2019 & Office of the Data Protection Commissioner (personal data collection, consent, storage).

### Success Criteria

By the end of this session, Duffy should have:

1. A clear, justified **entity-type recommendation** for this stage (business name vs. limited company), with reasoning on cost/speed of registration, ability to register as a formal employer (PAYE/NSSF/SHIF), personal liability exposure for Duffy, and ease of converting/upgrading later.
2. A documented **checklist of personal info/documents** Hestia can lawfully collect from HCPs at onboarding, flagging which ones (birth certificate, ID, DCI Certificate of Good Conduct) require explicit consent or extra safeguards under the Data Protection Act 2019.
3. A **draft outline of the HCP onboarding policy/agreement** (employment contract + data consent clause) ready to be turned into a document HCPs sign before starting.
4. A **"this week" action plan** that lets Duffy proceed with onboarding the first paid HCPs with materially reduced legal exposure, plus a short list of items flagged for a quick professional check (advocate / ODPC registration / KRA).
